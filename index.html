Why I Choose Coaching

A personal journey toward meaningful connection

My path to coaching began with my own transformation as a client. After years of achieving professional milestones and navigating complex organizational challenges, I discovered something profound: the conversations that changed me most weren't about strategy or optimization—they were about being truly seen and understood.

"I realized that beneath all our secular pursuits lies a fundamental human need to be witnessed, to be heard, and to know that someone genuinely cares about our flourishing."

This revelation ignited something I couldn't ignore. While I'm grateful for the analytical skills and strategic thinking developed through my professional journey, what truly energizes me is creating space for the kind of deep conversations that honor both our ambitions and our humanity.

Starting fresh in coaching feels both vulnerable and authentic. It's a return to what has always been truest about me: an almost overwhelming desire to help others flourish, paired with a deep belief in the transformative power of compassionate attention.

Meet Jill

Where experience meets heart

Here's my story: Stanford University (Master's), Renmin University (BBA & BS), startup chaos, corporate boardrooms, Silicon Valley madness. The whole journey from Beijing to Palo Alto and everywhere in between.

I've built strategies, led teams, navigated acquisitions, pivoted products. I speak English and Mandarin, which means I've learned to read between the lines in multiple cultures. I've been the person in the room translating not just languages, but entire ways of thinking.

But here's what all those experiences really taught me: success without connection feels hollow. Achievement without understanding feels empty. And sometimes the most strategic thing you can do is slow down and ask "What do I actually want?"

Like the characters in "The Best of Youth" — my favorite film — I believe our most profound growth happens through the relationships we build and the care we extend to one another. That Italian masterpiece showed me that compassion isn't soft. It's revolutionary.

So yes, I bring all that corporate experience to our conversations. But more importantly, I bring curiosity. Genuine care. And the absolute belief that you already have everything you need — sometimes you just need someone to help you see it.
